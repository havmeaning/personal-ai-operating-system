# LinkedIn Post — Paper Announcement

**AgentOS Frameworks in 2026: A Comparative Evaluation for Personal-Scale Knowledge Work**

---

Most knowledge workers are running 12 AI tools that don't talk to each other.
That's not intelligence. That's noise.

---

We are in the middle of an agent proliferation crisis.

Copilots. Summarisers. Research assistants. Code generators. Schedulers. Each one powerful in isolation—none of them aware that the others exist. Knowledge workers are left doing the integration work themselves, manually shuttling context between tools, rebuilding mental state from scratch with every new session, and absorbing the coordination overhead that AI was supposed to eliminate.

The problem isn't the individual tools. The problem is the absence of an operating system beneath them.

---

That's the core argument in my new paper: **AgentOS Frameworks in 2026: A Comparative Evaluation for Personal-Scale Knowledge Work**.

A Personal AI Operating System (PAIOS) isn't a smarter chatbot. It's the persistent, modular infrastructure that lets specialised agents share memory, coordinate tasks, and maintain continuity across a knowledge worker's entire cognitive workflow—the way an OS coordinates processes, not the way a single app replaces them.

---

**Key findings from the evaluation:**

• Current agent frameworks (LangChain, AutoGen, CrewAI, and others) were designed for enterprise pipelines or developer playgrounds—not for the nuanced, context-rich demands of personal knowledge work.

• The critical gap isn't reasoning capability. It's **persistent context**, **inter-agent memory**, and **modular composability**—the three properties that separate an OS from an app.

• Modular architectures consistently outperform monolithic ones on adaptability, maintainability, and long-term task coherence—especially when the knowledge worker's goals evolve over time.

• No current framework fully satisfies the PAIOS criteria. The architecture remains an open research problem.
